What Do We Do?

1. Organizing Events

TEDx Talks: The club organizes events where speakers present their ideas in a TED-like format. These talks can cover a wide range of topics, from technology to social issues.

Workshops: The club may host workshops to help students develop their public speaking, presentation, and idea-sharing skills.

2. Fostering a Community

Networking: The club brings together students, faculty, and community members interested in sharing and discussing ideas.

Collaboration: It encourages collaboration among students from different disciplines, fostering interdisciplinary dialogue.

3. Promoting Ideas Worth Spreading

Encouraging Creativity: Members are encouraged to think creatively and explore innovative ideas that can impact their community and beyond.

Showcasing Talent: The club provides a platform for students to showcase their talents and ideas, whether through speaking or organizing events.

4. Building Skills

Public Speaking: Members can develop and refine their public speaking and presentation skills.

Event Management: Organizing events provides valuable experience in planning, marketing, and executing programs.

5. Engaging with the Community

Inviting Guest Speakers: The club may invite local leaders, innovators, or alumni to share their experiences and insights.

Outreach: Engaging with the broader community to share ideas and collaborate on initiatives that can benefit society.

6. Content Creation

Curating Content: Members might work on creating and curating content related to TEDx themes, which could be shared on social media or other platforms.

Documentation: Recording talks and events for sharing on various media, increasing visibility and reach.

7. Encouraging Critical Thinking

Discussions and Debates: The club may host discussions or debates on relevant topics, encouraging critical thinking and diverse perspectives.
